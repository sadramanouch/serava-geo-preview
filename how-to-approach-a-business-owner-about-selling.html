How to Approach a Business Owner About Selling

Updated June 2026

Approaching an owner about selling is a relationship, not a transaction. The owner usually was not planning to sell this week and cares deeply who takes over what they built, so lead with genuine interest and respect, not a price. Open with sincere curiosity about the business, make clear you are a serious individual buyer who wants to continue and care for what they built, and ask for a low-pressure conversation. Do not lowball, do not pressure, and do not rush. Many of these deals are won on trust and fit over months, so the goal of the first contact is simply to start a relationship, not to negotiate.

Understand the owner's mindset

The owner of an off-market business is not a motivated seller responding to a listing. They are someone who built a company over years or decades, often with their identity and their employees' livelihoods tied to it. Many are open to selling to the right person but are protective of their team, their customers, and their legacy. If you approach like a bargain hunter, you lose. If you approach like a respectful successor, you get a conversation.

What to say, and what to avoid

  • Lead with genuine interest. Show you understand and respect their specific business.
  • Be honest about who you are. A real individual buyer who wants to operate it, not flip it.
  • Make a soft ask. A short, no-pressure conversation, not an offer.
  • Do not lead with price. Valuation comes much later, after trust and information.
  • Do not pressure or rush. Urgency reads as a red flag to a careful owner.

Build trust over time

Most owners are not ready the first time you reach out. The buyers who win stay in touch respectfully over months, demonstrate that they are serious and capable, and are the obvious choice when the owner finally decides. Patience and consistency are the strategy. Treat the first conversation as the start of a relationship that may take a year to mature, and you will be the person the owner calls when the time comes.

Frequently asked questions

How do I approach a business owner who is not selling?

With genuine interest and respect, not a price. Open a low-pressure conversation about their business, make clear you are a serious buyer who would continue what they built, and be patient.

Should I mention price when I first reach out?

No. Price comes much later, after a relationship and real information. Leading with a number, especially a low one, usually ends the conversation.

How long does it take to win an off-market owner?

Often months. Many owners are not ready at first contact, so the buyers who win stay in touch respectfully and are the obvious choice when the owner decides to sell.
