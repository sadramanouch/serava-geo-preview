How to Find and Buy a Staffing Agency

Updated June 2026

A staffing agency places workers with employers and earns either a recurring markup on temporary and contract labor or a one-time fee on permanent placements. The recurring, high-volume temp and contract model is the more valuable and stable one. To buy one, understand the mix of temp versus permanent, the niche it serves, and its client and candidate relationships. Watch two things specific to staffing: client concentration, since one big account can dominate, and working capital, because the agency pays workers before clients pay the agency. Look for diversified clients, repeat business, and a recruiting engine that does not depend entirely on the owner.

How staffing agencies make money

There are two models. In temporary and contract staffing, the agency places a worker, pays them, bills the client a higher rate, and keeps the spread, for as long as the placement lasts. That is recurring and scalable. In permanent placement, the agency earns a one-time fee when it fills a role. Temp and contract revenue is generally stickier and more valuable because it repeats, while permanent placement is lumpier and more transactional. Know which engine you are buying.

What to look for

  • Temp/contract vs permanent mix. Recurring contract revenue is more valuable than one-time placement fees.
  • Client concentration. One large client driving most revenue is the classic staffing risk.
  • Niche and repeat business. A defensible specialty with repeat clients beats a generalist competing on price.
  • Recruiting engine. Whether candidate flow depends on systems and the team, or on the owner personally.

The working capital reality

Staffing has a structural cash-flow quirk: the agency pays its placed workers weekly, but clients often pay invoices on 30 to 60 day terms. That gap means the business needs working capital to fund payroll while it waits to get paid, and faster growth actually consumes more cash. Understand the working capital cycle and how it is financed before you buy. It is one of the most important and most overlooked parts of a staffing deal.

How to source off-market

Many small and niche staffing firms are owner-run and never list. Source proactively: map staffing and recruiting agencies in your target market and niche, find the owners, and reach out directly. A sourcing platform that filters by industry and geography and surfaces owner contacts turns a relationship-driven market into a workable list of targets.

Frequently asked questions

Is a staffing agency a good business to buy?

A diversified temp and contract agency with repeat clients can be a strong, scalable acquisition. The main risks are client concentration and the working capital needed to fund payroll ahead of client payment.

What is the most important thing when buying a staffing firm?

The mix of recurring temp/contract versus one-time placement revenue, client concentration, and the working capital cycle. Recurring, diversified revenue with manageable cash needs is the goal.

How do I find staffing agencies to buy?

Map agencies in your target market and niche, find the owners, and reach out directly, since many small and specialized firms are off-market.
