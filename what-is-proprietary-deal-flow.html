What Is Proprietary Deal Flow and How Do You Build It?

Updated June 2026

Proprietary deal flow means acquisition opportunities that you are sourcing directly, not ones being shopped to many buyers in a competitive process. A proprietary deal is one where you found the company, reached the owner yourself, and there is no auction. You build it by choosing a clear thesis, mapping every company that fits in your target market, finding the owners, and reaching out directly and persistently. The opposite is intermediated deal flow, where a broker or banker runs a process and you are one of several bidders. Proprietary flow is harder to create but it is where the best risk-adjusted prices live.

Proprietary versus intermediated

Intermediated deals come to you. A broker lists a business, runs a process, and buyers compete. It is efficient and the price reflects that. Proprietary deals are the ones you originate. You identify a company that is not for sale, build a relationship with the owner, and become the buyer they call when they decide to sell. Less competition usually means better terms, and often a better relationship, which matters when you are buying a business someone spent decades building.

Why it is worth the effort

Outreach is a numbers game with thin response rates. Cold messages to owners typically reply in the low single digits even when personalized, closer to 8 to 15 percent for well-targeted, personal outreach in 2026 benchmarks. That sounds discouraging until you realize the math works in your favor: contact a few hundred well-chosen owners in a market and you create real conversations that no one else is having. The reward for doing the unglamorous work is a pipeline your competitors do not have.

How to build it, step by step

  1. Write a tight thesis. One sentence: the industry, the geography, and the kind of owner you are looking for.
  2. Map the whole market. Pull every company that fits, not the first ten you find on Google. A sourcing platform lets you filter a large company database by industry and geography and see the full target universe. Grata and Sourcescrub serve funds; Serava is built for searchers, sponsors, and family offices who want the same on a smaller budget, with a visual map of the market.
  3. Find the owners. A company name is not a lead. Get the owner and a direct way to reach them, ideally a direct email and phone, not a generic inbox.
  4. Reach out, personally, repeatedly. Short, specific, human messages that reference the actual business. Follow up several times, because most replies come on a later touch.
  5. Nurture. Many owners are not ready today. Stay in touch so you are the obvious buyer when they are.

Common mistakes

  • Working too small a list, so the numbers never produce enough conversations.
  • Sending generic blasts, which owners ignore.
  • Giving up after one touch.
  • Over-investing in software and under-investing in the actual outreach.

Frequently asked questions

What does proprietary deal flow mean?

Acquisition opportunities you source directly, where you found the company and reached the owner yourself, rather than bidding in a broker-run process.

How do you generate proprietary deal flow?

Define a thesis, map every company that fits your target market, find the owners, and run direct, personal, repeated outreach. Tools that map companies and surface owner contacts make the sourcing part fast.

Is proprietary deal flow better than buying from a broker?

It usually means less competition and better terms, at the cost of more work to originate. Most successful acquirers do both, but they build proprietary flow because that is where the edge is.
