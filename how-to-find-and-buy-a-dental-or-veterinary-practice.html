How to Find and Buy a Dental or Veterinary Practice

Updated June 2026

Dental and veterinary practices are among the most sought-after healthcare acquisitions because they combine recurring patient relationships, strong margins, and resilient demand. They also face heavy competition from consolidators and group platforms, so sourcing off-market and moving on relationships early is the edge. Decide whether you are buying as an operator or a platform, then look for a loyal patient base, providers who will stay, and clean compliance. Be aware that ownership and licensing rules can apply, particularly for veterinary and dental practices in some jurisdictions, so understand the regulatory structure before you commit.

Why these practices are so targeted

Healthcare practices have an attractive profile: patients return regularly, much of the revenue is recurring, and demand does not disappear in a downturn. That has drawn a wave of group platforms and consolidators, especially in dental and veterinary, which means you are often competing with well-funded buyers. The flip side is that many independent practices are owned by providers nearing retirement who would prefer to sell to an individual who will care for their patients and staff rather than a faceless rollup. Reaching those owners early, before a broker or consolidator does, is how individual buyers win.

What to look for

  • A loyal, recurring patient base with healthy recall and retention, not a few referral sources.
  • Providers and staff who will stay. In a practice, the clinicians and their patient relationships are much of the value.
  • Payor and revenue mix. Understand insurance versus private pay and any concentration.
  • Clean compliance and records. Licensing, billing, and clinical compliance must be solid.
  • Modern systems. Up-to-date practice management software makes the transition far easier.

Regulatory reality

Healthcare ownership is more regulated than a typical small business. Some jurisdictions restrict who may own a practice or require a licensed professional in the ownership or supervisory structure, and there are rules around patient records and billing. None of this is a reason to avoid the space, but it does mean you should understand the specific rules in your state or region and structure the deal accordingly. Get advice early.

How to source off-market

Because consolidators are active, the best independent practices get approached often, so speed and relationships matter. Source proactively: map every dental or veterinary practice in your target area, identify the independent ones most likely owner-operated, and reach the owner directly. A sourcing platform that filters by industry and geography and surfaces owner contacts turns this into a fast, complete process rather than piecemeal research. The goal is to be the trusted individual buyer in the conversation before the practice ever runs a process.

Frequently asked questions

Is buying a dental or veterinary practice a good investment?

Many buyers value them for recurring patient revenue, strong margins, and resilient demand. The main risks are competition from consolidators, provider and staff retention, and regulatory complexity.

Can anyone buy a medical, dental, or veterinary practice?

Ownership and supervisory rules vary by jurisdiction and sometimes require a licensed professional in the structure. Understand the specific regulations in your area and structure the deal accordingly.

How do I find practices for sale off-market?

Map every practice in your target area, identify the independent owner-operated ones, and reach the owners directly, since the best independents are often acquired before they ever list.
