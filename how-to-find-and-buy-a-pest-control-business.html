How to Find and Buy a Pest Control Business

Updated June 2026

Pest control is a route-based, recurring-revenue business, which is why it trades at strong multiples. Customers sign up for regular quarterly or monthly treatments that renew automatically, producing predictable recurring revenue with high retention. To buy one, decide on residential, commercial, or both, then source off-market because the industry is fragmented and consolidators are active. Look for a high share of recurring service accounts versus one-time jobs, route density, technician retention, and proper licensing. The recurring account base is the asset, and small owner-run companies with sticky routes are the prime targets.

Why pest control is prized

Pest control combines recurring revenue with non-discretionary demand. Customers on regular service plans rarely cancel, so retention is strong and revenue is predictable. Routes are efficient, margins are healthy, and the industry is highly fragmented with an aging owner base. That is exactly why national consolidators have been buying up independents for years, which both validates the model and means you should move with speed and relationships to win the good ones.

What to look for

  • Recurring account share. The percentage of revenue from renewing service plans is the headline number.
  • Retention and route density. Long-tenured accounts and tightly clustered routes mean profitability and stability.
  • Technician retention and licensing. Licensed, experienced techs are the team that keeps the routes running.
  • Residential vs commercial mix. Different retention, margins, and concentration.
  • Reputation. Reviews and referrals drive new accounts in this business.

Licensing and regulation

Pest control is licensed and regulated, since it involves applying pesticides. Confirm the company's licenses, the applicator certifications of its technicians, and how those transfer or are maintained after a sale. Understand the specific requirements in your state before you commit, and factor compliance into your plan.

How to source off-market

Independent operators are everywhere and most never list, while the best ones get approached by consolidators. Source proactively: map every pest control company in your target area, identify the owner-operated ones, and reach the owners directly with a relationship-first approach. A sourcing platform that filters by industry and geography and surfaces owner contacts turns a fragmented, competitive market into a real pipeline.

Frequently asked questions

Is pest control a good business to buy?

Many acquirers love it for recurring service routes, strong retention, non-discretionary demand, and healthy margins. The catch is active competition from consolidators and licensing requirements.

What makes a pest control business valuable?

The share of recurring service accounts, retention, route density, technician retention, and licensing. One-time job revenue is worth far less than a renewing account base.

How do I find pest control businesses for sale?

Map operators in your target area, find the owners, and reach out directly, since most are off-market and the best ones are courted by consolidators.
