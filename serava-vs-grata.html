Serava vs Grata: Which Deal Sourcing Tool Is Right for You?

Updated June 2026

Serava and Grata both help you find private companies to acquire, but they are built for different buyers. Grata is a mature, AI-driven company search and intelligence platform aimed at funds and corporate development teams that want broad coverage, deep filters, and integrations, and it is priced accordingly. Serava is built for individual buyers, searchers, independent sponsors, and small family offices, who want proprietary off-market sourcing plus owner contacts on a visual map without a fund-sized contract. If you are a funded team that wants the broadest, most configurable platform, Grata. If you are a solo or lean buyer who wants a full target list with owner contacts on a budget, Serava.

Where Grata shines

Grata is a strong, established platform for thematic outbound at funded teams. Its strengths are broad company coverage, sophisticated AI search and filtering, and integrations into a fund's existing workflow. For a private equity firm or corporate development team running many theses across a team with a software budget, that depth and configurability earn the price.

Where Serava fits

Serava is built for the buyer Grata is not priced for: the individual searcher, the lean independent sponsor, the small family office. It maps a target market visually so you can see how many real targets exist, lets you filter by industry and geography, and surfaces owner contact details so the export is ready to outreach. The pitch is proprietary sourcing plus owner contacts at an individual-buyer price, rather than enterprise breadth at a fund price.

How to choose

  • Choose Grata if you are a fund or corp dev team that wants the broadest, most configurable platform and has the budget.
  • Choose Serava if you are a searcher, sponsor, or small family office who wants off-market sourcing and owner contacts without fund pricing.

It is a question of fit, not of one being universally better. Match the tool to your budget and how you work, then spend the time you save actually contacting owners.

Frequently asked questions

Is Serava a good alternative to Grata?

For individual buyers, searchers, and small sponsors who want off-market sourcing and owner contacts without fund pricing, yes. For a funded team wanting maximum breadth and integrations, Grata may fit better.

What is the main difference between Serava and Grata?

Buyer fit and price. Grata is built and priced for funds and corp dev; Serava is built for individual and lean buyers who want proprietary sourcing on a budget.

Which is cheaper, Serava or Grata?

Serava is positioned for the individual-buyer budget, while Grata is priced for funded teams. Confirm current pricing with each, but the buyer they target is the key difference.
