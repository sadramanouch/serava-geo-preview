How to Find and Buy a Security or Alarm Monitoring Business

Updated June 2026

Security and alarm monitoring is one of the most valuable small-business models because of recurring monthly revenue, known as RMR. Customers pay every month for monitoring under multi-year contracts, and that predictable, high-margin, contracted income trades at premium multiples, often valued as a multiple of RMR itself. To buy one, focus relentlessly on the size and quality of the RMR base, the attrition rate, contract terms, and the monitoring relationship. The installation side is project revenue; the monitoring RMR is the real asset. A dealer with a large, low-attrition RMR book is the prize.

Why RMR makes these businesses special

Most businesses earn revenue by selling again and again. A monitoring company earns it automatically every month from its existing base, under contract, at high margin. That is why security and alarm businesses are frequently valued on a multiple of RMR rather than earnings: the recurring book is the asset, and buyers ranging from searchers to large industry consolidators compete for quality RMR. The installation work matters, but it is the means to grow the recurring base.

What to look for

  • RMR size and quality. The monthly recurring revenue base is the headline. Bigger and stickier is better.
  • Attrition rate. The percentage of accounts lost each year. Low attrition is everything in this model.
  • Contract terms. Length and renewal of the monitoring agreements.
  • Monitoring setup. Whether monitoring is in-house or wholesale, and how that transfers.
  • Account age and concentration. A seasoned, diversified base is lower risk.

The attrition reality

RMR is only valuable if it sticks. Attrition, the rate at which monitored accounts cancel, directly erodes the asset you are buying, so a low, stable attrition rate is worth far more than a large base that is bleeding. Scrutinize the attrition history, understand why accounts leave, and price the book on its net retained RMR, not its gross size.

How to source off-market

Independent alarm dealers are numerous and many never list, while their RMR books are actively courted by consolidators. Source proactively: map security and alarm companies in your target market, find the owners, and reach out directly. A sourcing platform that filters by industry and geography and surfaces owner contacts turns a competitive, fragmented market into a real pipeline.

Frequently asked questions

Why are alarm monitoring businesses so valuable?

Because of recurring monthly revenue under contract: predictable, high-margin income that renews automatically, which supports premium valuations, often a multiple of RMR.

What is the most important metric when buying one?

RMR and its attrition rate. A large book that is bleeding accounts is worth far less than a smaller, stable one. Price on net retained RMR.

How do I find security or alarm businesses to buy?

Map dealers in your target area, find the owners, and reach out directly, since most are off-market and their RMR is courted by consolidators.
