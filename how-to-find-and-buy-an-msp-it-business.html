How to Find and Buy an MSP or IT Services Business

Updated June 2026

A managed service provider, or MSP, is valuable because of recurring contracts: clients pay a monthly fee to have their IT managed, which produces predictable monthly recurring revenue. The more of the revenue that is contracted and recurring versus one-time project work, the more the business is worth. To buy one, decide on a client size and industry focus, then source off-market because most owner-run MSPs never list. Look for a high share of monthly recurring revenue, low client concentration, strong contract terms, and technicians who will stay. Project-heavy IT shops are worth far less than true contracted MSPs, so know which one you are buying.

Why MSPs are sought after

The MSP model turned IT support from project work into a subscription. Instead of being paid per fix, the provider charges a recurring monthly fee to manage a client's technology. That creates sticky, predictable monthly recurring revenue, and switching providers is painful for clients, so retention tends to be strong. Add an aging base of founder-owners and a fragmented market, and MSPs have become a favorite for searchers and IT-focused consolidators.

What to look for

  • Monthly recurring revenue share. The percentage of revenue under contract is the headline number. Higher recurring means higher value.
  • Contract terms. Length, renewal, and pricing of the managed-services agreements.
  • Client concentration. A few large clients driving most revenue is a real risk.
  • Technician retention. Skilled engineers are scarce and hard to replace. The team is much of the value.
  • Tooling and documentation. A standardized, well-documented stack integrates far more easily than an ad-hoc one.

Recurring versus project revenue

Many IT businesses are a mix of contracted managed services and one-time projects, like installs and migrations. The contracted, recurring portion is what carries the value, because it is predictable and sticky. A shop that looks busy but bills mostly one-off projects is closer to a job than an asset. Separate the two when you evaluate and price the business.

How to source off-market

Most small MSPs are owner-run and never list. Source proactively: map every IT services and managed services provider in your target geography, identify the owner-operated ones, and reach out directly. A sourcing platform that filters by industry and location and surfaces owner contacts turns a fragmented technical market into a workable target list.

Frequently asked questions

Why are MSPs good businesses to buy?

Because managed-services contracts produce recurring monthly revenue with strong retention, since switching IT providers is disruptive. That predictable, sticky income supports strong valuations.

What is the most important metric when buying an MSP?

The share of monthly recurring revenue under contract, followed by client concentration, contract terms, and technician retention.

How do I find MSPs for sale?

Most are owner-run and off-market. Map every MSP and IT services provider in your target area, find the owners, and reach out directly rather than waiting for a listing.
