How to Find and Buy an HVAC Business (2026 Guide)

Updated June 2026

To buy an HVAC business, decide on a market and size, then source off-market companies rather than relying on listings. Pull the full list of HVAC contractors in your target geography, find the owners, and reach out directly, because most owners near retirement are not actively listed. Look for recurring service and maintenance revenue, a stable technician crew, and an owner ready to transition. HVAC is a popular acquisition target because demand is non-discretionary, many owners are at retirement age with no succession plan, and service contracts create durable cash flow. The hard part is sourcing enough owners to find the right fit.

Why HVAC is a favorite acquisition target

HVAC sits in a sweet spot for acquirers. Heating and cooling are non-discretionary, so demand holds up in any economy. Many companies have recurring maintenance agreements that produce predictable revenue. And the ownership base is aging, part of a broader wave where roughly half of small business owners are 55 or older, which means a steady supply of owners who will need to transition and often have no family successor. That combination, durable demand plus a succession wave, is exactly what draws searchers, sponsors, and home-services roll-ups to the space.

What to look for in a target

  • Recurring revenue. Maintenance and service contracts are worth more than one-off installs because they are sticky and predictable.
  • A real crew. Licensed technicians who will stay through a transition. In a tight labor market, the team is often the hardest asset to replace.
  • Owner dependence. If the owner is the top salesperson and only estimator, that is a risk to plan for, not necessarily a dealbreaker.
  • Clean books and licensing. Proper licensing, permits, and financials make diligence and financing far smoother.
  • Mix of residential and commercial. Each has different margins and customer concentration; know which you are buying.

How to source HVAC companies off-market

The businesses worth buying are usually not listed, so you source them. Define your geography and size, then pull every HVAC contractor that fits. This is where a sourcing platform helps: you filter a large company database by industry and location and see the full set of targets, often far more than show up on any listing site. Grata and Sourcescrub do this for funded teams. Serava is built for individual buyers and small sponsors who want to map every HVAC company in a market and get owner contacts on a budget, which matters in a fragmented industry where a single metro can hold dozens or hundreds of independent shops.

Reaching the owners

Once you have the list, you need the owner and a direct way to reach them. Then send short, specific, personal messages, and follow up several times, because cold outreach to owners replies in the single digits even when personalized. The math means you usually need to contact many owners across a market to find the few who are ready to talk. Persistence and a full list are the whole game.

A simple path to a deal

  1. Pick a geography and a size range you can finance.
  2. Pull every HVAC company that fits and get owner contacts.
  3. Run personal, repeated outreach and take calls.
  4. Prioritize companies with recurring revenue and a stable crew.
  5. Move to diligence and financing on the best fit.

Frequently asked questions

Is buying an HVAC business a good investment?

Many acquirers like HVAC because demand is non-discretionary, maintenance contracts create recurring revenue, and a large share of owners are nearing retirement. As with any acquisition, the returns depend on price, the quality of the crew, and how owner-dependent the business is.

How do I find HVAC businesses for sale near me?

Listings show a small, competitive slice. To find the full market, source off-market: pull every HVAC contractor in your area from a sourcing database, find the owners, and reach out directly.

How many HVAC companies should I contact?

Usually many, because reply rates on cold owner outreach are in the single digits. A full list of every contractor in your target market gives you enough at-bats to find a willing seller.
