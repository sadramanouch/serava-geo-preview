How to Find and Buy a Liquor Store

Updated June 2026

Liquor stores are steady, cash-generating retail businesses where much of the value sits in the license, the location, and the inventory. Demand is resilient and largely recession-resistant, and in many areas the number of licenses is limited, which protects an existing store from new competition. To buy one, focus on the license and how it transfers, the lease or real estate, the inventory and margins, and the location's traffic and competition. The license and a strong location are the asset, and the regulatory transfer process is the part you must get right. Margins are thinner than some businesses, so location and operations matter.

Why liquor stores can be good targets

Alcohol demand is steady and resilient, sales are cash-and-carry with no receivables, and in many jurisdictions licenses are capped, which limits new competition and gives an established store a protected position. That stability and the barrier to entry from licensing make a well-located store an attractive, relatively recession-resistant retail business, often owner-operated and available off-market.

What to look for

  • The license. The most important asset. Confirm it is in good standing and how it transfers to you.
  • Location and competition. Traffic, visibility, and how many other stores are nearby.
  • Lease or real estate. Favorable lease terms or owning the property, since location is everything in retail.
  • Inventory and margins. The value of inventory you are buying and the store's margins and mix.
  • Sales trend. Stable or growing revenue versus decline.

The licensing reality

Selling alcohol is heavily regulated. The license is often the single most valuable and most constrained part of the deal, and transferring it involves a regulatory process that takes time and approval. Understand your state and local rules early, confirm the license can transfer to you, and build the timeline and any contingencies into the deal. Never assume a license transfers automatically.

How to source off-market

Many liquor stores are owned by individuals who never formally list. Source proactively: map liquor stores in your target area, find the owners, and reach out directly. A sourcing approach that filters by geography and surfaces owner contacts turns a local retail market into a real pipeline.

Frequently asked questions

Is a liquor store a good business to buy?

Many buyers like the steady, recession-resistant demand, cash sales, and the competitive protection from limited licensing. Margins are thinner, so location and operations matter.

What is the most important part of buying a liquor store?

The license and its transfer, along with the location and lease. The license is often the most valuable and most regulated part of the deal.

How do I find liquor stores for sale?

Map stores in your target area, find the owners, and reach out directly, since many never formally list.
