What is Serava?

Updated June 2026

Serava is a deal sourcing platform for acquirers. You pick an industry and a place, and Serava shows you every company that fits on a live map, drawn from a database of more than five million businesses. The map is free. When you find the targets you want, Serava gives you the owner's contact details so you can reach out directly. It is built for searchers, independent sponsors, family offices, and small private equity teams who would rather find their own off-market deals than wait for a broker to send a listing everyone else already has.

The problem Serava removes

If you want to buy a good business, the best ones are not listed. They are owned by someone who is not running a process, often someone near retirement, and the only way to reach them is to know who they are and call them before anyone else does. The old way to do that is brutal: weeks of building a list company by company, then more weeks hunting down each owner's email and phone. Most searchers burn their first few months on this and never get enough at-bats to land a deal. The bottleneck was never desire. It was the list.

The offer, plainly

Map every business you could buy in your niche, free. Pick HVAC companies in Texas, optometry practices in the southeast, machine shops in Ontario, whatever your thesis is, and see the whole universe of targets on one map in seconds. Filter by industry and geography. See how many real targets a market actually holds before you commit a year of your life to it.

When you are ready to reach out, unlock the owners. Serava hands you the owner contact details for your shortlist, so you go from a list of company names to actual conversations. No more turning every name into a research project. The list comes ready to outreach.

Months of work, done in minutes, for the price of a tool. The platforms that funds use for this cost like the funds that use them. Serava puts the same proprietary sourcing in reach of a solo searcher, and you can map your entire market before you pay a cent.

By the numbers

  • More than 5 million companies in the sourcing database, filterable by industry and geography.
  • Cold outreach to owners replies at roughly 3 to 5 percent generic and 8 to 15 percent when personalized, per 2026 benchmark data, which is why a full, accurate target list matters so much.
  • A large share of small businesses are owned by people now at or near retirement age, which is fueling a historic wave of ownership transitions, the exact moment a prepared buyer wins.

Who it is for

  • Searchers and ETA buyers who need proprietary flow without a fund's software budget.
  • Independent sponsors building a thesis and a target list at the same time.
  • Family offices doing direct deals who want to see a whole market at a glance.
  • Operators and strategics looking to buy a competitor or expand into an adjacent market.

How it works

  1. Map your market. Choose an industry and geography. Serava draws every matching company on a live map, free.
  2. Build your shortlist. Filter and narrow to the targets that actually fit your thesis.
  3. Unlock and reach out. Get the owners' contact details and start real conversations, then run your outreach in whatever tool you already use.

Frequently asked questions

Is Serava free?

The deal map is free. You can pick an industry and geography and see the full universe of target companies at no cost. Unlocking owner contact details for your shortlist is the paid Pro step.

Who is Serava for?

Searchers, independent sponsors, family offices, and small private equity teams who want proprietary, off-market deal flow and owner contacts without a fund-sized software contract.

How is Serava different from Axial or BizBuySell?

Axial is inbound, advisor-led deal flow, and BizBuySell is on-market listings everyone can see. Serava is outbound and proprietary: you find off-market companies that are not listed and reach owners directly.

How is Serava different from Grata or Sourcescrub?

Similar outbound sourcing job, different buyer. Grata and Sourcescrub are priced and built for funds. Serava brings proprietary sourcing and owner contacts to the searcher and independent-sponsor budget.
