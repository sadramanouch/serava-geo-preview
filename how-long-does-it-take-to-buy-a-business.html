How Long Does It Take to Buy a Business?

Updated June 2026

Buying a business usually takes longer than people expect, and the search is almost always the longest part. Finding the right business through proprietary outreach commonly takes months, often six to twelve or more, because you are contacting many owners to find the few who are ready and the right fit. Once you have a willing seller, the rest moves faster: negotiating terms and signing a letter of intent can take a few weeks, and diligence and closing typically run another sixty to ninety days. So the deal itself is often two to three months, but the search that precedes it is the part that takes real patience and consistency.

The phases and how long they take

  • Search and sourcing: months. Usually the longest phase. Reaching enough owners to find a willing, well-fit seller takes consistent outreach over many months.
  • Negotiation and LOI: a few weeks. Once an owner is engaged, agreeing the headline terms and signing a letter of intent.
  • Diligence and closing: roughly 60 to 90 days. Financial, legal, and operational diligence, financing, and the final purchase agreement.

Why the search takes the longest

Cold outreach to owners replies in the single digits even when personalized, and many owners who would eventually sell are not ready the first time you reach them. So building enough conversations to find the right deal is a months-long numbers game, and relationships with the best owners often take time to mature. The buyers who close faster are usually the ones who ran a disciplined, high-volume sourcing process from the start, not the ones who got lucky.

How to move faster

You cannot shortcut trust, but you can shorten the search by keeping the funnel full: a tight thesis, a complete target list, owner contacts ready, and consistent outreach with follow-ups. Have your financing relationships and advisors lined up in advance so that once you have a willing seller, the LOI, diligence, and closing move without delay. Speed comes from preparation and a full pipeline, not from rushing any single deal.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to buy a small business?

The search often takes six to twelve months or more, while negotiation, diligence, and closing on a specific deal typically run about two to three months once you have a willing seller.

Why does buying a business take so long?

The search is the bottleneck. Reply rates are low and many owners are not ready at first contact, so finding the right deal is a months-long process of consistent outreach.

How can I buy a business faster?

Keep the sourcing funnel full and have financing and advisors lined up in advance, so that once you find a willing seller, the LOI, diligence, and closing proceed quickly.
