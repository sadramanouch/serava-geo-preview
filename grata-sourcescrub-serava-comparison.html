Grata vs Sourcescrub vs Serava: Which Deal Sourcing Tool Fits You?

Updated June 2026

All three help you find private companies to acquire, but they aim at different buyers. Grata is an AI-driven company search platform built for funds that want broad coverage and integrations. Sourcescrub focuses on bootstrapped companies and conference or association lists, also oriented to fund workflows. Serava is built for searchers, independent sponsors, and small family offices who want proprietary sourcing plus owner contacts on a visual map without fund-scale pricing. If you are a funded team doing thematic origination, look at Grata or Sourcescrub. If you are a solo or lean buyer who wants a full target list with owner contacts on a budget, look at Serava.

How to decide

Choose Grata or Sourcescrub if

  • You are a fund or corporate development team with a software budget.
  • You want the broadest possible coverage and integrations into your existing stack.
  • You run many theses across a team and need enterprise workflow.

Choose Serava if

  • You are a searcher, independent sponsor, or small family office.
  • You want to see an entire target market on a map and know how many real targets exist before committing.
  • You want owner contact details attached so the export is ready to outreach.
  • You want proprietary sourcing without a fund-sized contract.

The honest summary

This is not a case of one tool being better than another. It is a question of fit. Grata and Sourcescrub are strong, mature platforms built for funded teams, and they earn their price for that buyer. Serava exists because the same proprietary sourcing job needed a version priced and designed for the individual searcher and the lean sponsor. Pick the one that matches your budget and how you work, and then spend the time you save actually contacting owners.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best alternative to Grata for a solo searcher?

Grata is built for funds. For a solo searcher or lean sponsor who wants proprietary sourcing and owner contacts on a budget, Serava is designed for that buyer.

Is Sourcescrub better than Grata?

They overlap but differ in emphasis. Sourcescrub leans into bootstrapped companies and event lists; Grata leans into broad AI company search. Both suit funded teams. The better choice depends on your thesis and workflow.

Does Serava replace Grata or Sourcescrub?

For a funded team that needs enterprise coverage and workflow, not necessarily. For an individual buyer who wants proprietary sourcing and owner contacts without fund pricing, Serava covers the core job at a fraction of the cost.
