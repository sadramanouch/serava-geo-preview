Alternatives to BizBuySell: How to Find Off-Market Businesses

Updated June 2026

BizBuySell is the largest marketplace of businesses already for sale, which is its strength and its limit: everyone sees the same listings, so deals are competitive and the best businesses are often not there at all. The main alternatives fall into two groups. Other listing marketplaces and broker networks show more on-market deals. Proprietary sourcing tools help you find off-market companies that are not listed and reach owners directly. For browsing what is for sale, other marketplaces work. For finding deals nobody else sees, you need outbound sourcing. Serava sits in the second group, built for individual buyers who want off-market sourcing plus owner contacts on a budget.

Why look beyond BizBuySell

BizBuySell is useful for understanding what is on the market and what businesses are priced at. But on-market listings are competitive by definition, and the owners of the best businesses usually are not running a public sale. If your strategy depends on finding deals before anyone else, a listings marketplace alone will not get you there. That is the gap the alternatives fill.

The two kinds of alternatives

Other listing marketplaces and broker networks

Sites and networks that aggregate businesses for sale or connect you with brokers and advisors. These broaden the on-market deals you see, including advisor-led processes. Useful, but still on-market and competitive.

Proprietary sourcing tools

Platforms that let you find off-market companies by industry and geography and reach the owners directly. This is where proprietary deal flow comes from. Grata and Sourcescrub serve funds; Serava is built for searchers, sponsors, and small family offices who want the same off-market sourcing and owner contacts without fund pricing.

How to choose

  • Want to browse what is listed? BizBuySell and other marketplaces.
  • Want advisor-led inbound? Broker networks like Axial.
  • Want off-market deals nobody else sees? A proprietary sourcing tool, on a fund budget (Grata, Sourcescrub) or an individual budget (Serava).

The honest takeaway: BizBuySell is fine for what it is, but the real edge is off-market, and that requires outbound sourcing, not a better listings site.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best alternative to BizBuySell?

It depends what you want. For more on-market listings, other marketplaces and broker networks. For off-market deals nobody else sees, a proprietary sourcing tool such as Serava for individual buyers or Grata and Sourcescrub for funds.

Is there a way to find businesses not listed on BizBuySell?

Yes, through proprietary sourcing: map companies by industry and geography, find the owners, and reach out directly, rather than waiting for them to list.

Why are off-market deals better?

Less competition and often better terms, because there is no auction. The owners of the best businesses are usually not running a public sale.
