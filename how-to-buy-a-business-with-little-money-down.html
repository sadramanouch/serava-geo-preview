How to Buy a Business With Little Money Down

Updated June 2026

You can buy a business with less cash than most people think, but truly zero down is rare and usually unrealistic. The practical way to minimize your cash is to stack financing: an SBA or bank loan funds the bulk of the price, a seller note covers part of it so the seller is paid over time, and your equity injection fills the rest. The more the seller is willing to finance, the less cash you need at closing. Lenders still require some buyer equity and a business with cash flow that comfortably covers the debt, so low money down depends on a strong, financeable business and a cooperative seller, not on a trick.

The realistic structures

Minimizing your cash is about combining sources rather than finding a zero-down loophole. The common low-cash structure pairs an SBA 7(a) loan for most of the price with a seller note for a meaningful portion and a modest buyer equity injection. A larger seller note directly reduces the cash you need. Some sellers, especially those eager to retire and confident in the business, will finance a significant share, which is what makes a low-money-down deal possible.

What it actually requires

  • A financeable business. Stable, verifiable cash flow that comfortably covers the debt. This is non-negotiable for a lender.
  • A cooperative seller. One willing to carry a meaningful note rather than demand all cash at close.
  • Some buyer equity. Lenders require it. Low down does not mean no down.
  • A credible operator. You need to be someone a lender and a seller trust to run it.

A note of realism

Be wary of zero-down promises. The math has to work: the business must service the debt and still pay you, and stacking too much debt onto a thin business is how deals fail. The goal is not the lowest possible cash at any cost, but a sound structure that lets you control a good business with a manageable amount of your own capital. A strong business plus a willing seller is what actually lowers the cash you need.

Frequently asked questions

Can you really buy a business with no money down?

Truly zero down is rare. You can minimize cash by stacking an SBA or bank loan with a seller note and a modest equity injection, but lenders still require some buyer equity.

What is the lowest-cash way to buy a business?

Combine an SBA or bank loan for most of the price with a large seller note and your minimum required equity. The more the seller finances, the less cash you need.

What makes low-money-down deals possible?

A business with strong, financeable cash flow and a seller willing to carry a meaningful note. It depends on the deal, not a trick.
