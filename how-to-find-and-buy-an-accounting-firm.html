How to Find and Buy an Accounting Firm (2026 Guide)

Updated June 2026

Buying an accounting or CPA firm is different from buying a trades business. The value is in recurring client relationships and the staff who serve them, so retention through the transition is everything. Decide on a location and size, then source firms off-market, because most retiring partners are not publicly listed. Look for sticky recurring work like tax and monthly bookkeeping, a client base that is not concentrated in a few accounts, and staff who will stay. Many firms transition through a multi-year partner buyout rather than a clean sale, so plan for an earnout and a handover period. The supply is large because a wave of partners is reaching retirement with no internal successor.

Why accounting firms are sought after

Accounting firms have qualities acquirers love. Tax and bookkeeping work is recurring and recession-resistant, clients are sticky because switching accountants is painful, and revenue is predictable. At the same time, the profession is aging, part of the broader wave where roughly half of small business owners are 55 or older, and many sole practitioners and small-partnership firms have no internal successor. That mismatch, durable demand and a retiring owner base, creates steady acquisition supply.

What to look for

  • Recurring vs project revenue. Monthly bookkeeping and annual tax relationships are worth more than one-time consulting projects.
  • Client concentration. A firm where one or two clients drive most revenue is riskier than a broad, diversified base.
  • Staff retention. The team and their client relationships are the asset. Losing key staff in a transition can erase the value.
  • Software and processes. Modern, documented workflows make integration far easier than a paper-and-spreadsheet shop.
  • Licensing and partner structure. Understand who holds the licenses and how the partnership is structured before you negotiate.

How these deals are usually structured

Accounting firm transitions often are not a clean cash sale on day one. A retiring partner frequently stays on for a transition period, and a portion of the price is tied to client retention through an earnout. This protects you, since the value walks out the door if clients leave, and it gives the seller a reason to hand off relationships properly. Expect to negotiate a handover, not just a closing.

Frequently asked questions

Is buying an accounting firm a good business?

Many acquirers like accounting firms for recurring, sticky revenue and predictable demand. The risk is client and staff retention through the transition, which is why deals often include earnouts and handover periods.

How do I find accounting firms for sale?

Listings are limited and competitive. To find the whole market, source off-market: pull every accounting or CPA firm in your target area, find the managing partner, and reach out directly.

How are accounting firm acquisitions structured?

Often as a partner transition with an earnout tied to client retention and a handover period, rather than a single cash payment, because the value depends on clients and staff staying.
