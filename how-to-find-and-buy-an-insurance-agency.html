How to Find and Buy an Insurance Agency

Updated June 2026

An independent insurance agency is one of the most prized small-business acquisitions because it is built on renewal commissions: a book of policies that renews every year and pays the agency a recurring cut. That recurring, sticky income is why agencies often sell at higher multiples than typical small businesses. To buy one, decide on personal lines, commercial lines, or both, then source off-market, since most agency owners are relationship-driven and never list. Look hard at retention, the renewal rate of the book, carrier relationships and contracts, and how dependent the relationships are on the retiring owner. The book of business is the asset, and its stickiness is everything.

Why agencies command a premium

Most small businesses sell on a multiple of earnings in the low single digits. Insurance agencies often command more, because the book of business renews. Customers rarely shop their insurance, so a well-run agency keeps the large majority of its policies year after year, and each renewal pays commission with little additional work. That recurring, high-retention income is rare in small business, and it is why agencies are pursued by searchers, larger agencies, and aggregators alike.

What to look for

  • Retention rate. The single most important number. A book that renews in the 90s is far more valuable than one bleeding clients.
  • Commission mix and carriers. Which carriers, what contracts, and whether those appointments transfer to you.
  • Personal vs commercial lines. Different economics, retention, and concentration. Know what you are buying.
  • Owner dependence. If the relationships live entirely with the retiring owner, retention is at risk in the transition.
  • Staff and service. The team that services renewals is part of why clients stay.

Carrier and transfer considerations

An agency's value depends on its carrier appointments and contracts continuing after the sale. Confirm early that the carrier relationships and any required appointments can transfer to you or your entity, and understand any licensing requirements. A book of business is only as good as your ability to keep writing and renewing it under the same carriers.

How to source off-market

Agency sales are quiet and relationship-driven, so the best ones rarely hit a public listing. Source proactively: map every independent agency in your target market, identify the owner-operated ones likely facing succession, and reach out directly. A sourcing platform that filters by industry and geography and surfaces owner contacts turns a discreet, fragmented market into a real pipeline.

Frequently asked questions

Why are insurance agencies valuable to buy?

Because of renewal commissions. The book of business renews each year with high retention, producing recurring, sticky income that is rare in small business, which supports higher multiples.

What matters most when buying an agency?

Retention rate, carrier relationships and whether they transfer, the personal-versus-commercial mix, and how dependent the book is on the departing owner.

How do I find insurance agencies for sale?

Most sell off-market and quietly. Map independent agencies in your area, find the owners, and reach out directly, since the best ones are rarely publicly listed.
