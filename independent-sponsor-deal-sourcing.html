Independent Sponsor Deal Sourcing: How to Find Deals Without a Fund

Updated June 2026

Independent sponsors raise capital deal by deal, so a proprietary deal is the entire pitch to investors. The sourcing job is the same as a searcher's but the stakes are higher: you need a compelling, off-market opportunity in hand before you can raise. The process is to pick a thesis you can credibly execute, map every company that fits, reach owners directly, and bring a proprietary deal to your capital partners. Because you do not have a committed fund, the quality and exclusivity of the deal you source is what wins both the company and the money.

Why sourcing matters more for a sponsor

A fund has committed capital and can chase intermediated deals. An independent sponsor has neither, so a competitive, broker-run auction is a hard place to win, because you are raising equity at the same time you are bidding. A proprietary, off-market deal solves both problems at once. There is no auction to lose, and a unique opportunity is far easier to fund. In practice, your edge as a sponsor is the deal you found that nobody else saw.

The sourcing process

  1. Pick a thesis you can sell. Investors back sponsors who clearly understand a space. Choose an industry and angle you can speak to credibly.
  2. Map the market. Pull every company that fits your thesis by industry and geography, so you are working the whole field, not a handful of names. A sourcing platform makes this fast. Grata and Sourcescrub serve funded teams; Serava is built for sponsors who want proprietary sourcing and owner contacts without a fund-scale contract.
  3. Reach owners directly. Get the owner and a direct line, then run personal, repeated outreach. Reply rates are single digits even when personalized, so you need real volume to surface a willing seller.
  4. Bring the deal to capital. Once you have a proprietary opportunity and a relationship with the owner, you have the asset that lets you raise.

What investors want to see

  • A proprietary deal, not a competitive process everyone else is bidding on.
  • A sponsor who clearly knows the industry and the specific company.
  • A direct relationship with the owner, not an intermediated introduction.

All three come from doing the sourcing yourself. That is why, for an independent sponsor, sourcing is not a step. It is the business.

Frequently asked questions

How do independent sponsors find deals?

Mostly through proprietary outbound sourcing: choosing a thesis, mapping companies by industry and geography, and reaching owners directly, because a proprietary deal is what lets a fundless sponsor raise capital.

What is the best sourcing tool for an independent sponsor?

One that maps a target market and surfaces owner contacts without fund pricing. Serava is built for that buyer; Grata and Sourcescrub suit larger funded teams.

Can you do a deal without a committed fund?

Yes, that is the independent sponsor model. You source and structure a proprietary deal first, then raise the equity for that specific transaction.
