How to Find and Buy a Pool Service Business

Updated June 2026

Pool service is a route-based, recurring-revenue business: homeowners pay a monthly fee for regular cleaning and chemical maintenance, and those accounts renew season after season. That recurring book, plus higher-margin repair and equipment work, is the appeal. To buy one, focus on the share of recurring maintenance accounts, route density, and customer retention, and understand seasonality, which is strong in cold climates and milder in warm ones. The recurring account base and tight routes are the asset. A business that is mostly one-time repairs is worth far less than one with a loyal maintenance book.

Why pool service is attractive

Recurring maintenance accounts make pool service predictable and sticky. Customers rarely cancel a working service, routes are efficient, and repair and equipment replacement add higher-margin revenue on top. The industry is fragmented and full of owner-operators, and in warm-climate markets demand is close to year-round. That recurring, route-based model is exactly what searchers and home-services consolidators look for.

What to look for

  • Recurring account share. The number of monthly maintenance accounts is the core value driver.
  • Route density. Tightly clustered accounts are far more profitable than scattered stops.
  • Retention. Long-tenured accounts and low churn signal a healthy book.
  • Repair and equipment revenue. Higher-margin work layered on the maintenance base.
  • Technician retention. Reliable techs keep the routes and relationships intact.

The seasonality factor

In cold climates, pool service is seasonal, busy in spring through fall and quiet in winter, with openings and closings as additional revenue. In warm climates it runs closer to year-round. Understand the seasonal pattern in your market, value the business on a full annual cycle rather than a peak month, and plan working capital for the slow season.

How to source off-market

Independent pool service companies are everywhere and most never list. Source proactively: map operators in your target area, find the owners, and reach out directly. A sourcing platform that filters by industry and geography and surfaces owner contacts turns a fragmented market into a workable list of recurring-revenue targets.

Frequently asked questions

Is pool service a good business to buy?

Yes for many buyers: recurring maintenance routes, sticky customers, and higher-margin repair work, in a fragmented market. The main consideration is seasonality in colder climates.

What makes a pool service business valuable?

The share of recurring monthly accounts, route density, retention, and repair revenue. A repair-only business without a maintenance book is worth much less.

How do I find pool service businesses to buy?

Map operators in your area, find the owners, and reach out directly, since most are off-market and never list.
