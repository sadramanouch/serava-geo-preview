How to Build an Acquisition Thesis and Buy Box

Updated June 2026

A buy box, or acquisition thesis, is the clear definition of what you are trying to buy: the industry, the geography, the size, and the business characteristics that fit you. A good thesis fits in a sentence, like "profitable HVAC companies in the southeast with an owner near retirement." It does two crucial things. It tells you exactly which companies to source, so you work a defined market instead of wandering. And it makes your outreach credible, because owners can tell you understand their specific business. A vague thesis produces a vague list and generic emails that get ignored. A sharp one produces a tight list and messages that get replies.

What a buy box should include

  • Industry. A specific sector you can credibly understand and operate.
  • Geography. Where you will buy, narrow enough to work the full market.
  • Size. A revenue or earnings range you can finance and run.
  • Business characteristics. Recurring revenue, low customer concentration, an owner ready to transition, transferable operations.
  • Deal breakers. What you will not buy, which is as useful as what you will.

Why a tight thesis wins

A sharp thesis is leverage. It turns sourcing from a vague hunt into a defined task: pull every company that fits, work the whole market, and know your numbers. It also transforms outreach. When you message an owner and clearly understand their industry and their kind of business, you sound like a real buyer who did the work, not a tourist firing off a template. Credibility lifts reply rates, and a defined market lets you actually finish sourcing it.

How to choose your thesis

Start from your strengths and what you can credibly operate and fund. Favor fragmented industries with recurring or sticky revenue and an aging owner base, since those produce the most off-market opportunities. Keep it narrow enough that you can map the entire market and contact every owner in it. You can always broaden later, but a thesis you can say in one sentence is the sign you are ready to source.
