Best Free Deal Sourcing Tools for Buying a Business

Updated June 2026

You can start sourcing acquisitions for free, but free tools cover different jobs. To browse on-market listings, BizBuySell and similar marketplaces are free to search. To map off-market companies, Serava has a free tier where you can see the full universe of targets by industry and geography on a map, with paid Pro to unlock owner contacts. General free tools like Google Maps, LinkedIn, and state business registries help you find and research individual companies and owners by hand. The honest rule is that finding companies can be free, but getting accurate owner contacts at scale is where you eventually pay, because that data is the expensive part.

Free for browsing listings

BizBuySell and other business-for-sale marketplaces are free to search. They only show on-market listings, which are competitive, but they are a good free way to understand pricing and what is available in your area.

Free for mapping off-market targets

The bigger opportunity is off-market, and you can start there for free too. Serava offers a free deal map where you choose an industry and geography and see every company that fits, so you can size a market and build a target list at no cost. The paid step is unlocking owner contact details for outreach. This freemium split is common in sourcing tools: seeing the companies is free, reaching the owners is the paid value.

Free general tools for manual research

  • Google and Google Maps to find companies in an area and read reviews and basics.
  • LinkedIn to identify owners and key people and understand a company.
  • State and county business registries for registered names, officers, and addresses, usually free to search.
  • Company websites for direct contact details when they are published.

These cost nothing but your time, and time is the catch. Researching one owner by hand is free and easy. Researching two hundred is a part-time job, which is why buyers eventually move to a tool that attaches owner contacts to a full list.

Where free runs out

Free tiers and manual research are perfect for getting started, sizing a market, and contacting a handful of owners. The wall you hit is scale. When your strategy depends on reaching a whole market of owners with accurate, direct contact details, manual lookup becomes the bottleneck and verified contact data is the thing worth paying for. Start free, prove the approach, and upgrade the specific part that is slowing you down.

Frequently asked questions

Is there a free way to find businesses to buy?

Yes. Marketplaces like BizBuySell are free to browse for on-market listings, Serava has a free deal map for off-market companies, and free tools like Google Maps, LinkedIn, and state registries help you research individual companies and owners.

What is the catch with free deal sourcing tools?

Finding companies can be free, but getting accurate owner contact details at scale is the part you usually pay for, because that data is expensive to assemble and verify.

How do I start sourcing on a budget?

Use free listings and a free deal map to size your market and build a target list, do manual research on your first handful of owners, then pay only for the bottleneck, which is usually owner contacts at scale.
