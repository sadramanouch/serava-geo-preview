Quality of Earnings Explained for Buyers

Updated June 2026

A quality of earnings review, or QoE, is a financial deep-dive that an accountant performs to confirm a business actually earns what the seller claims, before you buy it. Because price is usually a multiple of earnings, a QoE protects the most important number in the deal. It verifies the reported earnings against tax returns and bank records, scrutinizes the add-backs sellers use to adjust profit, checks for one-time or non-recurring items, and assesses how stable and sustainable the earnings really are. For anything beyond a very small deal, a QoE is worth the cost, because a single overstated add-back, multiplied by the deal multiple, can mean overpaying by far more than the review costs.

Why earnings quality matters so much

Small businesses are valued on a multiple of earnings, so the earnings figure is the foundation of the price. If that number is overstated, you overpay by the overstatement times the multiple. Sellers often present adjusted earnings with add-backs, profit plus the owner's salary and various personal or one-time expenses, and some of those adjustments are legitimate while others are aggressive. A QoE is how you separate real, sustainable earnings from an inflated number.

What a QoE examines

  • Verification. Tying reported earnings to tax returns and bank statements, not just a spreadsheet.
  • Add-backs. Testing each adjustment to see if it is legitimate and truly non-recurring.
  • One-time items. Removing unusual gains or expenses that will not repeat.
  • Sustainability. Whether the earnings are stable and transferable, or a one-year spike.
  • Working capital and trends. The cash the business needs and where the numbers are heading.

When to get one

For a very small deal you might rely on careful self-review and your own accountant's check. For anything more substantial, a formal QoE from an accountant experienced in acquisitions is worth the cost. It is also what lenders often want to see. Think of it as cheap insurance on the single most important number in your deal.

Frequently asked questions

What is a quality of earnings review?

A financial deep-dive by an accountant that verifies a business actually earns what the seller claims, by checking the reported earnings, add-backs, one-time items, and sustainability.

Do I need a QoE to buy a business?

For anything beyond a very small deal, yes. It protects the earnings figure your price is based on and is often what lenders want to see.

What is the difference between a QoE and an audit?

An audit checks compliance with accounting standards. A QoE focuses on whether the earnings are real, recurring, and sustainable, which is what matters for pricing an acquisition.
