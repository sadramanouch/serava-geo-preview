How Search Funds Find Companies to Buy (2026)

Updated June 2026

Search funds find companies two ways: proprietary outbound, where the searcher sources companies directly and contacts owners, and intermediated flow from business brokers and M&A advisors. Most successful searchers run both but lean on proprietary outreach, because broker deals are competitive and the best acquisitions are usually off-market. The practical process is to pick an industry, build the full list of companies that fit, find each owner's contact details, and send personal, repeated outreach. The searchers who close are the ones who keep that funnel full for months.

The two channels

Proprietary outbound

This is where most searchers spend their time. You define a thesis, pull a complete list of companies in that space, find the owners, and reach out directly. It is more work than waiting for listings, but it puts you in front of owners before any competition shows up. Sourcing platforms make the first part fast by letting you filter a large company database by industry and geography. Grata and Sourcescrub serve funded teams; Serava is built for self-funded searchers and lean operations that want proprietary sourcing and owner contacts without fund pricing.

Brokers and advisors

Business brokers and M&A advisors bring listed deals. Networks like Axial aggregate advisor-led processes. This flow is real and worth cultivating, but it is intermediated and competitive, so treat it as a supplement to your own sourcing.

What the daily work looks like

A searcher's week is mostly top-of-funnel. Refine the thesis, add companies to the list, enrich owner contacts, send a batch of personal messages, follow up on prior ones, and take calls with owners who respond. Reply rates are thin, in the single digits even when personalized, so volume and consistency matter more than any single clever email. The job is to never let the pipeline run dry.

A simple system

  • One clear thesis you can say in a sentence.
  • A sourcing tool to build and refresh the target list.
  • Owner contacts attached to each target.
  • A cold email tool plus phone for outreach, with several follow-ups per owner.
  • A simple tracker for replies and conversations.

The searchers who struggle are usually the ones with too small a list and too few touches. The ones who win treat sourcing like a system and run it every day.

Frequently asked questions

How do search funds source deals?

Mostly through proprietary outbound: choosing a thesis, building a full list of companies, finding owner contacts, and reaching out directly, supplemented by broker and advisor relationships.

How many companies does a searcher contact?

Often hundreds across a target market, because reply rates on cold owner outreach are in the single digits even when personalized. A real pipeline needs volume.

What tools do searchers use to find companies?

Sourcing platforms that filter a company database by industry and geography and surface owner contacts. Grata and Sourcescrub for funded teams, Serava for self-funded searchers on a budget, plus broker networks like Axial for inbound.
